places to visit

london bridge

my wish list for visiting

London Bridge is a popular landmark in London, the capital city of England, Northwestern Europe. And as the popular nursery rhyme "London Bridge is falling down" implies, the bridge never actually fell down in real life. It has 2 towers, in victorian gothic style. It is connected with the walkways and the machine below the base lifts up so that it can allow the water to pass within it.



eiffel tower

my wish list for visiting

Eiffel tower is a 1.83 feet tall tower (1.83 feet tall? Really? I know, right?) Constructed using wrought iron. It was named after Gustave Eiffel, who was also the one who designed it. It was once yellow and created during the French Revolution. You would be amazed to know that the Eiffel Tower moves (as it get affected by weather). It's located in Paris, the capital city of France, in Western Europe.



calgary tower

my wish list for visiting

The Calgary Tower is 190 meters tall free-standing observation tower in the downtown core of Calgary, Alberta, Canada, North America. It was built in the honour to celebrate Canada's centennial of 1967. The construction took 15 WHOLE MONTHS and was built to withstand serious weather conditions (betcha didn't know that.) In 1988, Calgary hosted "Winter Olympics" due to which cauldron was installed on the top of the tower and basically turned it into a HUGE torch.



Review on movies and TV Shows

Boss Baby
EXCELLENT

I haven't seen this film but it is very cool despite some of the viewing and I just look forward to seeing many other fun animations too. The animation in the movie is very realistic. The movie starts with a story of 2 siblings. A new baby arrives at the house of the Temple family, told from the point of view of a delightfully unreliable narrator -- a wild 7-year-old (and a half) named Tim (Full name is Timothy Leslie Templeton. Leslie? That's a girl name, dude!). The most unusual Boss Baby (voiced by Alec Baldwin) arrives at Tim's house in a cab, wearing a business tuxedo and carrying a suitcase. The instant sibling envy must soon be put aside when Tim discovers that Boss Baby is actually a business baby on an agenda, and only he can help assist a risky job that involves a skirmish between puppies and babies. Based on a popular book by Marla Frazee.

The Fixies: Top Secret
AWESOME

This 2017 film is based on a popular 2010 animated series from Russia. It is based on a classic tale by a Russian author. The film is about fixies, tiny humanlike creatures who live in machines and appliances. They fix machines and appliances with backpack-like objects from pack-o-mats from which they can summon any tool. But they have to keep their existence a secret from humans. Now they have a human friend named Tom-Thomas (Full name is Thomas Wearington Jr. Possibly named after his father.). And he has promised to keep them a secret. That becomes hard. When one of the fixie classmates transforms into a super-powered rascal, it is up to his fixie friends, Tom-Thomas, and a lab professor named Professor Eugenius to help save the day.

Peanuts: By Schulz (TV Series)
SUPER INSPIRING

The `Peanuts' comic strips were always a staple of daily life for many people around the world for almost 50 years until the original production finished in the 2000s. Now, the classic cartoons are coming to life -- in animated version at least -- in this series. The show uses a pen-and-ink animation type along with textured backgrounds and framing to emulate the looks of the original strips as they used to appear in paper. `Peanuts' aims to bring the classic adventures of Charlie Brown, Snoopy, Lucy, Linus and the rest of the gang to a new chapter that is many times as familiar with 2015's computer-animated `The Peanuts Movie' than the original graphic papers.

News about environment

  • LEGO plans to find replacement material for bricks by 2030

    In an effort to be more environmentally responsible, LEGO have said they aim to stop manufacturing bricks made of plastic resin and are considering other options, which could include hemp. According to Telegraph World, the company makes 60 billion blocks a year. While LEGO want to replace the harmful plastic used in their products, they also require the new material to meet the high standards that allow the blocks to work as they always have, especially with the effective “clicking” together is a key aspect of the iconic coloured block.

  • Amazon deforestation and number of fires show summer of 2019 not a 'normal' year

    The perceived scale of the Amazon blazes received global attention this summer. However, international concerns raised at the time were countered by the Brazilian Government, which claimed the fire situation in August was 'normal' and 'below the historical average'.An international team of scientists writing in the journal Global Change Biology say the number of active fires in August was actually three times higher than in 2018 and the highest number since 2010.
